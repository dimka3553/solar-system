Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect ball of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core. The Sun radiates this energy mainly as light, ultraviolet, and infrared radiation, and is the most important source of energy for life on Earth.

Surface temperature: 5,778 K

Distance to Earth: 149.6 million km

Age: 4.603 billion years

Radius: 696,340 km

Mass: 1.989 × 10^30 kg

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun. Its orbit around the Sun takes 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all the Sun's planets.

Surface temperature: 167°C

Distance from Sun: 58 million km

Orbital period: 88 days

Radius: 2,439.7 km

Mass: 3.285 × 10^23 kg

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is sometimes called Earth's "sister" or "twin" planet as it is almost as large and has a similar composition. As an interior planet to Earth, Venus appears in Earth's sky never far from the Sun, either as morning star or evening star.

Surface temperature: 464°C

Distance from Sun: 108.2 million km

Orbital period: 243 days

Radius: 6,051.8 km

Mass: 4.867 × 10^24 kg

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. While large volumes of water can be found throughout the Solar System, only Earth sustains liquid surface water. About 71% of Earth's surface is made up of the ocean, dwarfing Earth's polar ice, lakes, and rivers.

Surface temperature: 13.9°C

Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km

Orbital period: 365 days

Radius: 6,371 km

Mass: 5.972 × 10^24

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury. In the English language, Mars is named for the Roman god of war.

Surface temperature: -62.8°C

Distance from Sun: 222.78 million km

Orbital period: 687 days

Radius: 2,439.7 km

Mass: 6.39 × 10^23 kg

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined, but slightly less than one-thousandth the mass of the Sun.

Surface temperature: -110°C

Distance from Sun: 741.09 million km

Orbital period: 4,333 days (12 years)

Radius: 69,911 km

Mass: 1.898 × 10^27 kg

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. It is a gas giant with an average radius of about nine and a half times that of Earth. It has only one-eighth the average density of Earth; however, with its larger volume, Saturn is over 95 times more massive. It has a massive ring system.

Surface temperature: -140°C

Distance from Sun: 1.434 billion km

Orbital period: 10,756 days (29.4 years)

Radius: 58,232 km

Mass: 5.683 × 10^26 kg

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. Its name is a reference to the Greek god of the sky, Uranus, who, according to Greek mythology, was the great-grandfather of Ares, grandfather of Zeus and father of Cronus. It has the third-largest planetary radius and fourth-largest planetary mass in the Solar System.

Surface temperature: -195°C

Distance from Sun: 2.871 billion km

Orbital period: 30,687 days (84 years)

Radius: 25,362 km

Mass: 8.681 × 10^25 kg

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the farthest known solar planet. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet. It is 17 times the mass of Earth, and slightly more massive than its near-twin Uranus.

Surface temperature: -200°C

Distance from Sun: 4.495 billion km

Orbital period: 165 years

Radius: 24,622 km

Mass: 1.024 × 10^26 kg

Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper belt, a ring of bodies beyond the orbit of Neptune. It is the ninth-largest and tenth-most-massive known object to directly orbit the Sun. It is the largest known trans-Neptunian object by volume, by a small margin, but is slightly less massive than Eris.

Surface temperature: -232°C

Distance from Sun: 5.910 billion km

Orbital period: 248 years

Radius: 1,188.3 km

Mass: 1.30 × 10^22 kg
